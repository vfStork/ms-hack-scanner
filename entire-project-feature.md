
# Hackathon Brief  
## Scan and Describe Differences

---

## Challenge Overview
Physical assets change over time, but their digital representations often lag behind. This challenge focuses on creating a solution that keeps a digital twin aligned with the real-world object by detecting, describing, and recording physical changes.

Your mission is to build a system that can scan an object, create an enriched digital twin, and reliably identify and document differences when the object changes.

---

## Problem to Solve
Digital twins are costly to maintain because even small physical changes require manual updates. Without automation, digital representations quickly become outdated, reducing their usefulness for inspection, maintenance, and decision-making.

---

## Goal
Create an end-to-end workflow that:
- Builds an initial digital twin from a physical object  
- Enriches it with meaningful descriptive data  
- Tracks physical changes over time  
- Keeps the digital twin accurate through automated updates  

---

## Required Features

### 1. Initial Digital Twin Creation
- Capture the geometry of a physical object  
- Produce a clean, usable digital representation  
- Store the digital twin as a baseline version  

### 2. Digital Twin Enrichment
- Automatically analyze the object and attach descriptive metadata  
- Include attributes such as:
  - Estimated material  
  - Type or classification of the object or its components  
  - Expected lifespan or wear characteristics  

### 3. Unique Identity & Versioning
- Assign a unique identifier to each digital twin  
- Maintain version history as the object evolves  

### 4. Change Detection
- Allow the physical object to be modified  
- Re-scan the object after changes  
- Detect and isolate differences between the original and updated versions  

### 5. Change Description & Logging
- Generate a human-readable description of what changed  
- Record when the change occurred and what was affected  
- Update the digital twin to reflect the new state  

---

## Demo Expectations
During the demo, participants should clearly show:
- The original physical object and its digital twin  
- The modified physical object  
- The updated digital twin with highlighted differences  
- A clear explanation of what changed and how it was recorded  

---

## Success Criteria
A successful project will demonstrate:
- Accurate detection of physical changes  
- Clear, understandable descriptions of differences  
- A digital twin that stays aligned with the real object over time  
- A workflow that reduces manual effort to keep digital models up to date
